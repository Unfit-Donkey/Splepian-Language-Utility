Numbers in Splepian

All Numbers begin with an "s" and end with a "yp". Start with an "s", append the characters of the numeral in base ten, then end with a "yp".

Innards

  • 0 - er
  • 1 - on
  • 2 - ot
  • 3 - etr
  • 4 - our
  • 5 - ib
  • 6 - ick
  • 7 - eb
  • 8 - ok
  • 9 - in
  • Negative - en
  • Period - op

Examples:

  • 64 - sickouryp
  • 1.5 - sonopibyp
  • -3 - senotryp

Verbs Conjugation in Splepian

Infinitive: no suffix
All other verbs are conjugated with two suffixes. First: the completion, second: the time.

Completion

  • Simple (do): ep
  • Continuous (is doing): ef
  • Perfect (has done): el
  • Perfect Continuous (has been doing): em

Time

  • Past: idy
  • Present: ity
  • Future: ify

Examples

  • I am - skip sepity
  • I am collecting (slope) - skip slopefity
  • I have started - skip sytopelidy
  • I will have been walking - skip sbibemify
  • I have said - skip splodelity

Splepian Grammar Rules

  • Adjectives go before nouns
  • Translative words go before (Example: sop sclirip means snowy)
  • The same word order and verb rules as English
  • Plural nouns and pronouns have the suffix "ity" (Example: sbebipity means bees)
